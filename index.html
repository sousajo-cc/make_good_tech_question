<?AskABetterQuestion version="1.0" goal="less-waste"?>

<AskTechnicalQuestion>

<Context>
Please sum up what you already did. What you've tried. The command(s) that you used, the error message(s), and the full logs or a path to them.
</Context>

<AvoidScreenshots>
While screenshots might help and give some context to the question, please remember one cannot copy text from them, and they are not searchable.
</AvoidScreenshots>

<Readability>
Use markdown. Makes the question/example more readable and easy to reason about.
</Readability>

<NoEnvironmentAssumptions>
Post full commands. People are not familiar with your aliases, .bashrc, and if you do rely on some local environment assumptions please mention those and how should they be reproduced.
</NoEnvironmentAssumptions>

<MinimalReproducibleExample>
It really helps to narrow down the source of the problem, and helps the people trying to to help you. Also, it shows that you invested time and effort on understanding it.
</MinimalReproducibleExample>

<Search>
Your question might already have been asked and answered. Take time to search it, before firing it up.
</Search>

<RightForum>
"Is this the right forum?"; Pose that question to yourself. Asking for help on a C++ or AWS issue on movies.stackexchange might not be...ideal.
</RightForum>

</AskTechnicalQuestion>

Less waste and better communication. How can we better help each-other.
